Whenever you visit a health and care service, care professionals must keep records about you. These records contain identifiable health and care information, like your name or NHS number.

Using your data

The NHS may use your identifiable health and care information in two different ways.

Your individual care

Health and care professionals access your identifiable health and care information when providing you with treatment or care.

Research and planning

Your identifiable health and care information is also used to:

  • plan essential facilities like heart units
  • improve the health of future generations
  • research potential cures for life-threatening diseases
  • develop treatment for serious illnesses

Where you have a choice

If you don't agree with health and care professionals using your identifiable health and care information for research and planning, you can opt out of this. The decision you make will not affect your individual care.
