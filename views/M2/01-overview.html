Your confidential patient information

Whenever you visit a health and care service, care professionals must keep records about you. These records contain identifiable health and care information, like your name or NHS number.

Your health and care records contain confidential patient information. This information can:

  • identify who you are, and
  • includes confidential information about your health, care and treatment

Examples include your name and NHS number, along with any conditions you have been diagnosed with or information about the social care services you receive.

Using your confidential patient information

Your confidential patient information is used in two different ways:

Your individual care

Health and care professionals access your confidential patient information when providing you with treatment or care.

For example, if you visit a hospital, the doctor you see may access your GP records for important information on your health.

Research and planning

Confidential patient information is also used to:

  • develop new ways to diagnose health conditions earlier
  • research and develop treatments for serious illnesses
  • plan health and social care services for the future

Where you have a choice

Your confidential patient information will still be used to support your own individual care. Any preference you set using this service will not change this.

However, if you don't agree with your confidential patient information being used for research and planning, you can opt out of this. If you do opt out, there are some specific situations where your data may still be used.

Setting your preference

You may use our online service, or request a print-and-post form, to set or change your preference at any time. You can also set a preference or request assistance using our telephone service. If you do not wish to opt out, you don't have to do anything at all.

You may also set a preference on behalf of another individual by proxy. For example, a parent may want to set a preference on behalf of a child under the age of 13.
