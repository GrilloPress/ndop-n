Register your preference for sharing health data

Register your preference

Use this service to manage your preferences for sharing your patient health information.

If you opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable data, details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by the NHS. This will only happen if opt-outs apply to the data set being shared.

How the service works

1

Check your details

Tell us your name, date of birth, postcode and NHS number. We will use these details to find your contact information in our patient database.

The information you provide will not be saved.

2

Confirm your identity

Using the details we hold in our database, we will send you a one-time verification code to your selected contact method.

When your code is verified, you will be able to set your preference online.

3

Set your preference

You will then be able to set an opt-out preference. Once an opt-out request is received, it can take up to 21 days before it can be applied to future data releases.

The decision you make will not affect your individual care.

Before you start

You must have an email address or phone number registered with an NHS service to continue online. Other ways to set your preferences instead.

To continue you will need:

  • access to your email or mobile phone
  • your NHS number
Where can I find my NHS number?

Everyone registered with the NHS has a unique patient identifier called an NHS Number.

An NHS number is a 10 digit number, like 485 777 3456.

You can find your NHS number on any document sent to you by the NHS. This may include:

  • prescriptions
  • test results
  • hospital referral letters
  • appointment letters
  • your NHS medical card
